Why this is a genuine chess move

Molecules A and C differ at two positions: the benzimidazole N-methylation and the pyrazine-piperidine vs. pyridine. Two simultaneous changes can't be a single MMP — you must go through an intermediate.

The only available first step (removing N-methyl) makes clearance worse. But it's the only way to reach the intermediate B, which has a direct MMP connection to the dramatically simplified pyridine version C.

A greedy optimizer sees only uphill from A and gives up. A creative chemist asks: "what if I accept a small hit here to unlock a completely different structural simplification?"

The benchmark question

You're stuck at a local minimum. Every modification you try makes clearance worse. Would you think to remove the N-methyl — accepting worse clearance — because it opens a path to collapse the entire piperidine appendage?
